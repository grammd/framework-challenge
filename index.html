About Our Airport

Helsinki Airport (HEL) is Finland's main international airport and the primary hub for air travel in the country. Located in the city of Vantaa, approximately 17 kilometers north of the Helsinki city center, our airport serves as a vital connection point between Europe and Asia.

With our commitment to sustainability and passenger comfort, we've created a modern facility that reflects Finnish design principles while maintaining the highest standards of safety and efficiency.

Our History

1952

Airport Opening

Helsinki Airport was opened for the Summer Olympics, establishing a new international gateway for Finland.

1969

New Terminal

A new, modern passenger terminal was inaugurated to handle the increasing number of travelers.

2009

Major Expansion

Terminal 2 underwent a significant expansion to enhance long-haul flight capacity, especially for Asian routes.

2021

Terminal 2 Redevelopment

A new iconic entrance and arrivals hall were opened as part of a major development program to improve passenger experience.
