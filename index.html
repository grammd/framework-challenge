About Our Airport

Helsinki Airport (HEL) is Finland's main international airport and a major hub for air travel in the region. Located in Vantaa, 17 kilometers north of Helsinki, HEL connects Europe to Asia.

The Helsinki-Vantaa Airport prioritizes elegance and passenger comfort, with modern facilities that reflect a clean and functional aesthetic.

Our History

1952

Airport Opening

Helsinki Airport officially opened its doors, coinciding with the Helsinki Summer Olympics and becoming Finland's primary international gateway.

1969

Terminal 1

First major terminal expansion completed, designed by Finnish architect Viljo Revell, featuring the iconic glass facade.

1993

Terminal 2

Second terminal opened to accommodate growing passenger numbers and international traffic.

2022

Major Renovation

Completed extensive renovation project including new check-in areas, security facilities, and enhanced passenger experience.
