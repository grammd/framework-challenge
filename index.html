About Our Airport

Helsinki Airport (HEL) is Finland's main international airport and a major hub for air travel in the Nordic region. Located in Vantaa, just 17 kilometers north of Helsinki, our airport serves as the primary gateway to Finland and a key connection point between Europe and Asia.

With our commitment to Finnish design excellence and passenger comfort, we've created a modern facility that reflects the clean, functional aesthetic that Finland is known for worldwide.

Our History

1952

Airport Opening

Helsinki Airport officially opened its doors, coinciding with the Helsinki Summer Olympics and becoming Finland's primary international gateway.

1969

Terminal 1

First major terminal expansion completed, designed by Finnish architect Viljo Revell, featuring the iconic glass facade.

1993

Terminal 2

Second terminal opened to accommodate growing passenger numbers and international traffic.

2022

Major Renovation

Completed extensive renovation project including new check-in areas, security facilities, and enhanced passenger experience.
